Our Story

The Prayer, The Stranger, and the Promise

In December 2021, I knelt down in prayer with one clear request: “Lord, let me meet my husband in 2022.” I didn’t know how it would happen, or where, or when. I only knew that I believed it would.

January 2022 arrived, and I was in full-blown mission mode — not for love, but for leadership. I was on the hunt for new members for my cell group at church, fueled by purpose, not romance. That Sunday, while scanning the congregation, my eyes landed on two young men seated quietly at the back. One of them had a quiet confidence about him — calm, observant, slightly mysterious.

I walked over and boldly asked for their numbers. We were still talking when a woman walked in and declared she had brought them to church. I politely stepped aside. No sparks, no magic — just another church connection… or so I thought.
His name was Charlse.
After that day, we kept in touch occasionally. He was persistent, but in a calm, oddly prophetic way. “You’ll be my wife,” he said more than once, as though he was simply stating a fact the universe had already agreed on.

I wasn’t impressed — not because he wasn’t charming, but because I wasn’t looking. My focus was razor-sharp: career, success, and checking off every goal I had set. I was a workaholic with a calendar full of meetings, not weddings. But Charlse? He was patient. He didn’t chase — he waited. And waited. And somewhere along the way, his quiet declarations started to feel less absurd, and more… destined.
By 2023, what started as a casual connection had evolved into courtship. Not the kind filled with butterflies and Instagrammable dates — but one grounded in friendship, faith, and a surprising sense of peace. It was as if everything had been orchestrated in advance, and we were just catching up to a story that had already been written.

And then, in January 2024 — just two years after that first unexpected meeting — I married the man who once sat quietly in the back of a church while I was too busy building to notice he was an answer to my prayer.
People always ask, “How did you know he was the one?”
I smile and say, “I didn’t. But God did.”
It was God Ordained
